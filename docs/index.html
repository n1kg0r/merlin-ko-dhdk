THE IDEA

Merlin is a mythical figure featured mainly in the Matter of Britain. He is usually depicted as a mage, a prophet and the advisor and mentor of King Arthur. He firstly appeared in Geoffrey of Monmouth’s Prothetiae Merlini and Historia Regum Britanniae as Merlinus Ambrosius. It results of the combination of two prophets featuring Briton legends, namely Visit W3Schools!Myrddin and Ambrosius. From Great Britain the legends of King Arthur and his court spread between French authors and then all over Europe, resulting in a wider yet multifaceted image of Merlin, which led to the creation of one of the prominent important figures in the imaginary and literature of the Middle Ages. Merlin’s traditional biography casts him as a cambion, the child of a woman and an incubus, from whom he inherits his magical powers. He engineers the arise of Arthur’s kingdom, becoming his advisor and mentor. One of the main magical features of him is the gift of prophecy, which inspired a wide variety of predictions. Merlin and stories involving his character have continued to be popular from the Renaissance to the present day, especially since the renewed interest in the legend of Arthur in modern times, when many other characters as been shaped on his model (i.e. Gandalf and Dumbledore). Moreover, the figure of Merlin appears in pop culture as much as in academic research, making it an interesting field both for the general public and the specialist scholars.

THE ITEMS

Because of his popularity, Merlin is the subject of countless cultural objects spreading over one thousand years of history. This project aims to depict the evolution, the differences and the resemblances in this tradition through ten exemplar items that can witness the development of this character through the centuries.

Merlijn, a letterpress printing of Merlin

This small printing made by Michel Wolgemut and Wilhelm Pleydenwurff illustrates a XV century copy of the Liber Chronicarum by Hartmann Schedel. The item is preserved at the Rijksmuseum of Amsterdam.
